Experience

I'm fourth-year Computer Science student at Oregon State University with a focus in Human Computer Interaction and minors in Psychology and Math.

Currently, I am an Undergraduate Developer at TAHMO Project, which aims to create a network of weather stations in Africa and utilize this weather data with machine learning algorithms to provide insight on agricultural, climate monitoring, and hydro-meteorological applications. I work on the ticketing system that handles improving weather stations utilizing Angular.JS, MongoDB, and IBM Cloud.

I am also an undergraduate research assistant in the Data Interaction and Visualization (DIV) Lab headed by Dr. Minsuk Kahng. We focus on creating data visualization tools to help people learn, explore, and understand machine-learning models. In this lab, I have experience with designing, creating, and evaluating these interfaces. Projects I have worked on include: an interface to find and improve the discrepancies between training and testing data for machine learning algorithms and creating an interface for users to easily create video highlights utilizing text and image classification models.

In Summer 2020, I was a Software Engineer Intern at Oracle on the Shepherd Team. I got to integrate a Slack application with Oracle Cloud's deployment system to immediately notify users about their automated deployment's status updated using Java, SQL, and Slack APIs.

At Oregon State, I was previously an IMPACT Lead Volunteer, Society of Women Engineers Ambassador, Project X Volunteer, undergraduate research assistant in the CARVE Lab, Honors College Web Editor, and College of Engineering Peer Tutor for CS & Math.
